# Case Template v1.0

**Document Type:** Structural Template  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Canonical

---

## Template Purpose

This document defines the ONLY permitted structure for cases within this documentation system. It is a structural template, not a case. It contains no content, no examples, and no illustrative material.

---

## Mandatory Constraints

All cases created using this template MUST operate in **non-claim mode**:

- No conclusions
- No recommendations
- No causation claims
- No predictions
- No optimization suggestions
- No normative language
- No generalizations beyond observed context

---

## Section 1: Context

### What MAY be written:

- Description of the system or environment under observation
- Observable facts about procedures, artifacts, and configurations
- Temporal markers indicating when observation occurred
- Role limitations of the observer

### What MUST NOT be written:

- Interpretation of why the situation exists
- Evaluation of adequacy or performance
- Attribution of responsibility
- Speculation about future states

---

## Section 2: What Can Be Explained

### What MAY be written:

- Observable patterns in the system
- Structural relationships between components
- Boundary conditions under which the model applies
- Descriptions of what is visible without inference

### What MUST NOT be written:

- Causal claims about why patterns exist
- Predictions about what will happen
- Recommendations for action
- Evaluations of effectiveness

---

## Section 3: Where Explanation Ends

### What MAY be written:

- The specific point at which the model loses applicability
- Conditions under which additional assumptions would be required
- Statement that semantic validity becomes indeterminate
- Acknowledgment of model limits

### What MUST NOT be written:

- Claims about what happens beyond the boundary
- Predictions of failure or success
- Recommendations for extending the model
- Causal explanations for the boundary location

---

## Section 4: Why No Further Claim Is Made

### What MAY be written:

- **Scope limitation:** Statement that the case is bounded by design
- **Evidence constraint:** Statement that available evidence supports description only
- **Authority boundary:** Statement that the observer lacks mandate for normative claims
- **Reversibility principle:** Statement that avoiding claims preserves future reassessment

### What MUST NOT be written:

- Justifications that imply hidden knowledge
- Suggestions that claims could be made with more data
- Recommendations for obtaining additional authority
- Evaluations of the adequacy of current evidence

---

## Section 5: Illustrative Status

### What MAY be written:

- Explicit statement: "This case is illustrative only"
- Statement that the case does not assert failure, inadequacy, or recommendations
- Statement that the case demonstrates structure, not outcomes

### What MUST NOT be written:

- Implications that the case reveals hidden truths
- Suggestions that the illustrative status is temporary
- Claims about what the case "really shows"
- Recommendations derived from the illustration

---

## Section 6: Links to Doctrine Boundaries

### What MAY be written:

- Reference to Scope page
- Reference to Boundaries page
- Reference to Evidence page
- Internal links using relative paths

### What MUST NOT be written:

- Interpretations of what linked pages mean for this case
- Claims that linked pages validate this case
- Recommendations based on linked content
- Summaries of linked page content

---

## Section 7: Case Classification

### What MAY be written:

- **Case Type:** Non-claim, illustrative
- **Boundary Level:** [Specify: Explanatory applicability / Structural / Procedural]
- **Claim Status:** None

### What MUST NOT be written:

- Evaluations of case quality or completeness
- Comparisons with other cases
- Recommendations for classification changes
- Claims about what the classification implies

---

## Template Enforcement Rules

1. Any case that contains conclusions is INVALID.
2. Any case that contains recommendations is INVALID.
3. Any case that contains causal claims is INVALID.
4. Any case that contains predictions is INVALID.
5. Any case that contains normative language is INVALID.
6. Any case that generalizes beyond observed context is INVALID.
7. Any case that lacks explicit illustrative status is INVALID.
8. Any case that lacks boundary acknowledgment is INVALID.
